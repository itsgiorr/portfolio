About Me

Hello! I'm Jorge Urdaneta, a Venezuelan-born mechanical engineer at UT driven by curiosity, challenge, and impact. Moving to the U.S. at 15, I've navigated cultures, led student organizations, managed projects, and I'm building an autonomous flying ATV. My mindset is to always try to grow and find a way to give back.

My passion is robotics: designing autonomous systems that make life easier, safer, and better for people. Whether that is in the shape of a car that keeps you safe, or a humanoid that assists you, I want to be the one to build it and change someone's life.

I pursue every project with purpose, grateful for the doors the world has opened for me throughout my path. I'm determined to repay the generosity and kidness of the world through meaningful innovation that will impact lives for ages.

Outside of academics, leading, and robotics, I'm passionate about exploring the world and expressing myself creatively. I enjoy soccer, hiking, trying new foods, travelling, fashion, and painting shoes. I'm always looking for ways to combine style, culture, and expressing myself.

I truly believe personality can take you a long way.
